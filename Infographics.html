PHONES






The journey of phones began with the invention of the telephone in the late 19th century.
Alexander Graham Bell, an inventor, teacher, and scientist, is credited with inventing the telephone in 1876.
His breakthrough allowed voice communication over long distances, replacing the telegraph as the primary method
of long-distance communication. Early telephones were large, bulky devices with limited reach,
but they marked the birth of the telecommunication revolution.
